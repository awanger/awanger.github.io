Ideation

Carson’s research explores achievement in ten creative domains. The original questionnaire is divided into ten sections accordingly. At the beginning of each section, the user is first asked if they have any training or recognize talent for that domain. If so, they are instructed to fill out the rest of that section. If not, they are instructed to go to the next domain. Majority (if not at all) users won’t need to fill out every section. The below diagram illustrates the inefficiency of the original form.

New Flow

To reduce the amount of time the user spent filling out the form. Instead of having the user go through all ten domains, the user is asked upfront which of the domains they have experience in. They are only asked questions pertaining to the domains they selected; essentially the irrelevant sections are hidden from them.

Visual Design

For the visual design, I used Adobe XD and went for a bubbly feel, using bright colors and round text. I wanted to turn the pencil and paper experience into something more fun and interactive.

Early Prototype

The initial prototype had a little helper character that I ended up scrapping because it took too much screen space. I also had to redo the whole interface as it was lacking visual hierarchy.
